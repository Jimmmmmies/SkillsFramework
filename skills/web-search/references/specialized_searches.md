# Specialized Search Scenarios

## Academic Research Searches

### Finding Scholarly Articles
**Best Sources:**
- Google Scholar (`scholar.google.com`)
- PubMed (`pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov`) - biomedical
- arXiv (`arxiv.org`) - physics, math, CS, etc.
- JSTOR (`jstor.org`) - humanities, social sciences
- IEEE Xplore (`ieeexplore.ieee.org`) - engineering
- ACM Digital Library (`dl.acm.org`) - computer science
- ScienceDirect (`sciencedirect.com`)
- SpringerLink (`link.springer.com`)

**Search Strategies:**
```
"exact phrase" author:"last name" year:2020..2024
topic AND (review OR survey) after:2020
"research gap" OR "future directions" intitle:topic
systematic review OR meta-analysis topic
```

**Citation Searching:**
- Use "Cited by" feature in Google Scholar
- Search for papers that cite key articles
- Look for review articles that summarize the field

### Finding Theses and Dissertations
- ProQuest Dissertations & Theses
- EThOS (British Library)
- DART-Europe
- Networked Digital Library of Theses and Dissertations (NDLTD)

### Finding Conference Proceedings
- Conference websites (often have proceedings)
- IEEE/ACM digital libraries
- ResearchGate and Academia.edu
- University institutional repositories

## Specialized Search Tips by Domain

### For Technical Problems
1. Include exact error messages in quotes
2. Specify software versions
3. Include operating system
4. Search forums and Q&A sites

### For Academic Topics
1. Use discipline-specific databases
2. Include publication years
3. Search for review articles first
4. Follow citation trails

### For Current Events
1. Use news aggregators
2. Check multiple sources
3. Verify with official statements
4. Be aware of time zones for breaking news

### For Historical Research
1. Use archival search tools
2. Include date ranges
3. Search for primary sources
4. Check digitized collections

### For Business Research
1. Use financial databases
2. Check regulatory filings
3. Look for industry reports
4. Analyze competitor information

## Cross-Domain Search Strategies

### When You Don't Know Where to Start
1. Begin with Wikipedia for overview
2. Identify key terms and concepts
3. Find review articles or surveys
4. Locate experts in the field
5. Follow references and citations

### When Information is Scarce
1. Try broader search terms
2. Look for related topics
3. Search in different languages
4. Check alternative spellings
5. Contact experts directly

### When Overwhelmed with Results
1. Add more specific terms
2. Use date filters
3. Limit to specific domains
4. Sort by relevance/date
5. Use "site:" operator for trusted sources

Remember: The right search strategy depends on both what you're looking for and why you need it. Always tailor your approach to your specific information needs.
